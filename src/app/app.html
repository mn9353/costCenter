Performance Hub — Cost Management Guide

What is this page?

Think of this dashboard as a control center for Crown Manufacturing's spending. It gathers all of our spending accounts in one place and lets us compare our actual spending against our forecasts. Because it's read-only, it's completely safe to click around, toggle things, and test views without altering any real records!

What does RFC mean?

RFC stands for Revised Forecast Scenario. A forecast isn't set in stone. As the year goes by, we replace our initial guesses with actual results and update our plans:

RFC 1 (March)

We look at what we actually spent in the first 3 months (Jan-Mar), and adjust our guesses for the remaining 9 months.

RFC 2 (June)

Our mid-year check. We now have 6 full months of real spending data, allowing us to make better predictions for the second half of the year.

RFC 3 (September)

With 9 months of real data done, we have a very clear picture of the year and update our estimates for the final 3 months.

RFC 4 (December)

Our final forecast update right before we close the books and lock in the year's results.

What are H1 and H2?

We split the year in half to make it easier to track progress:

  • H1 (First Half): Covers January 1st to June 30th. Once H1 ends, we perform our major mid-year RFC 2 forecast update.
  • H2 (Second Half): Covers July 1st to December 31st. During H2, controllers monitor the spending closely to ensure we hit our annual targets.

How the Forecasts (RFCs) are Calculated

An RFC is not just a guess. It is a formula that combines real spending (Actuals) with our remaining plans (Forecasts) as the year progresses:

RFC 1 (March Review)
3 Months Actuals + 9 Months Forecast

We take the actual money spent from January to March, and combine it with our original guesses for April through December.

RFC 2 (June Mid-Year)
6 Months Actuals + 6 Months Forecast

Our crucial mid-year review. We use the real spending from January to June, and adjust our plans for the second half of the year.

RFC 3 (September Review)
9 Months Actuals + 3 Months Forecast

We have a very clear picture by autumn. We use 9 months of real data and only forecast the final 3 months of the year.

RFC 4 (December Close)
12 Months Actuals + 0 Months Forecast

Right before we close the year. This is almost entirely actual data, locking in the final results.

Understanding Variance & the Comparison Columns (V1, V2, V3)

Variance measures the difference between two scenario columns to see if we are on track, overspending, or saving money:

Variance = Left Column - Right Column
+12k

Favorable Variance (Green): We spent less than our forecast. This represents cost savings!

-5k

Unfavorable Variance (Red): We spent more than our forecast. This represents an overspend.

On the dashboard, we have three predefined variance columns (V1, V2, and V3) to help analyze performance across different cycles:

V1 — Forecast Accuracy Test

Actual 2026 vs RFC 3 2025

Compares our final spending in 2026 with the forecast we set in late 2025. It answers: "How accurate was our forecast from a year ago?"

V2 — Yearly Baseline Shift

Actual 2025 vs RFC 1 2026

Compares our final spending in 2025 with our initial target for 2026. It answers: "How does our new target compare to what we actually spent last year?"

V3 — Two-Year Trend Check

Actual 2024 vs RFC 1 2026

Compares our actual spending from two years ago with our current targets. It answers: "Are we expanding or reducing our budgets over a longer two-year cycle?"

Key Features of the Dashboard

Here is a quick summary of the features and tools you can interact with on the dashboard:

  • Rearrange Columns: You can drag and drop forecast columns left or right to customize your view. A blue marker guides you where they will land.
  • Add a New Scenario: Use the "+ Add Scenario" button to add a new forecast type and year. The new column appears instantly, pre-filled with realistic currency amounts.
  • Zebra Layout with Frozen Columns: When scrolling horizontally, the left metadata columns stay frozen to keep your view organized. On mobile devices, only the "Account" column remains frozen to maximize the screen space.
  • Interactive Filters: Selecting a different Site or Team dynamically filters the rows and updates all summary totals and variance performance badges in real time.
  • Variance Performance Highlights: Clear green and red badges show budget performance at a glance. Green indicates budget savings (under-spend), and red indicates an overspend.
